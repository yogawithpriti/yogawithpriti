The Patanjali Tradition

Compiled around 400 CE by the sage Patanjali, the Yoga Sutras outline the eight limbs (Ashtanga) of yoga. Modern practice often focuses solely on Asana (physical postures), but true yoga encompasses ethical discipline, breath control, and deep meditation.

Our space is dedicated to preserving the sanctity of these Indian roots. We guide you not just to bend the body, but to observe the mind, clear energetic blockages, and align with your highest self.

Sacred Practices

Hatha Yoga

The foundational practice. 'Ha' (Sun) and 'Tha' (Moon) represent the balance of opposing energies. This slower-paced class focuses on holding postures to build strength, alignment, and deep inner stillness.

Vinyasa Flow

A dynamic evolution of traditional postures where movement is intimately synchronized with the breath (Pranayama). It creates a moving meditation that builds heat and purifies the body.

Ashtanga Vinyasa

A rigorous, traditional sequence passed down by Sri K. Pattabhi Jois. It involves synchronizing the breath with a progressive series of postures to produce intense internal heat and a strong, purified body.

Dhyana: The Art of Stillness

In the Patanjali Yoga Sutras, Dhyana (meditation) is the continuous flow of cognition toward the object of focus. It is the state where the observer, the process of observation, and the object being observed merge into complete awareness.

Take a moment to center yourself before you move on. Sync your breath with the expanding and contracting circle below to experience a brief moment of mindful stillness.
